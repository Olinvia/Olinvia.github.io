Multivariate Linear Regression

$$
h_\theta(x)= \theta_0 +\theta_1x_1+\theta_2x_2+…+\theta_nx_x
$$

For convenience of notation, define x0 = 1.(x0(i) =1)
$$
x=
\begin{bmatrix}
x_0\
x_1\
x_2\
.\
.\
x_n
\end{bmatrix}
$$

$$
\theta=
\begin{bmatrix}
\theta_0\
\theta_1\
\theta_2\
.\
.\
\theta_n
\end{bmatrix}
$$

$$
h_\theta = \theta^T x
$$

Algorithm

Repeat{

​
$$
\theta_j :=\theta_j - \alpha \frac{1}{m}\sum_{i=1}^m(h_\theta(x^{(i)})-y^{(i)})x_j^{(i)}
$$
(simultaneously update $\theta$j for j =0,1,2,3,…,n)

}

Feature Scaling

Get every feature into approximately a -3$\leqslant$x$ \leqslant$3 / -$\frac{1}{3}$$\leqslant$ x < $\frac{1}{3}$ range.

Replace xi with xi - $\mu$i to make features have approximately zero mean.

$x$ $\Rightarrow$ $\frac{x-\mu}{s}$

$\mu$ = average

s = max - min

Debug

Choose the right $\alpha$ .

Polynomial Regression

$$
h_\theta(x) = \theta_0+\theta_1x+\theta_2x^2+\theta_3x^3
=\theta_0+\theta_1x+\theta_2x_2+\theta_3x_3
$$

Pay attention to feature scaling.

Normal Equation

X m*(n+1)

Y m*1

(m is the number of training examples, n is the number of features)
$$
\theta = (X^TX)^{-1}X^Ty
$$

$$
X =
\begin{bmatrix}
…\
(X^{i})^T\
…
\end{bmatrix}
$$

No need to do feature scaling.

Slow if n is very large(bigger than 10000).
